FPI severity scale

Fine
FPI 0 — rates flat or falling. Teal blip on radar.
Feeling It
FPI 1-29 — rates up modestly. Teal blip, FPI shown on venue page.
Struggling
FPI 30-59 — rates up 50-100%. Solid amber blip. "Under Pressure" badge.
Fucked
FPI 60-99 — rates roughly doubled. Pulsing amber blip. "At Risk" warning flickers on lock-on.
Absolutely Fucked
FPI 100+ — extreme pressure. Pulsing purple blip. "In Serious Trouble" warning.

How it works in the app

📡

Radar

Toggle Troubled Pub Mode in the filter sheet. At-risk pubs appear as amber blips — pulsing harder the worse the FPI score.

🧭

Lock on

Tap an amber blip. The compass needle turns amber. A flickering warning appears: UNDER PRESSURE, AT RISK, or IN SERIOUS TROUBLE.

📊

Venue detail

Every pub page shows FPI score, severity label, and rateable value change — with a source credit linking back to ismypubfucked.com.

Attribution

How FPI data appears in PINtPOINT

Every venue showing FPI data includes a source attribution line linking directly to ismypubfucked.com. The severity labels (Fine, Feeling It, Struggling, Fucked, Absolutely Fucked) are preserved from your original categories.

Current coverage

PINtPOINT currently covers ~150 London pubs with FPI data matched by VOA address. The app is focused on Central London craft beer venues — a subset of your full 46,000+ dataset, but one where the FPI message hits hardest: drinkers standing outside a pub that might not be there next year.
